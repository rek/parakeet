## Volume Dose-Response

**Schoenfeld et al. 2017** — "Dose-response relationship between weekly resistance training volume and increases in muscle mass." *Journal of Sports Sciences*, 35(11). **Meta-analysis** (15 studies, 34 treatment groups). Each additional set associated with ~0.37% greater hypertrophy. 10+ sets/week significantly outperformed <5 sets/week.
- Used in: [volume-landmarks.md](volume-landmarks.md)
- https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/27433992/

**Pelland et al. 2025** — "The Resistance Training Dose Response: Meta-Regressions Exploring Weekly Volume and Frequency." *Sports Medicine*. **Meta-regression** (67 studies, 2,058 subjects, 220 hypertrophy + 490 strength effects). Fractional set counting method validated. Square root dose-response model (diminishing returns). For strength, diminishing returns are steeper than for hypertrophy.
- Used in: [volume-landmarks.md](volume-landmarks.md), [muscle-mapping.md](muscle-mapping.md)
- https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41343037/

**Baz-Valle et al. 2022** — "Effects of Different Resistance Training Volumes on Muscle Hypertrophy." *JSCR*. **Systematic review with meta-analysis** (7 studies). Recommends 12-20 weekly sets for trained men. Triceps responded to 20+ sets; biceps/quads showed no benefit beyond 12-20.
- Used in: [volume-landmarks.md](volume-landmarks.md)
- https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/35291645/

**Krieger 2010** — "Single vs. multiple sets for muscle hypertrophy." *JSCR*. **Meta-analysis** (8 studies). Multiple sets produced 40% greater hypertrophy ES. Trend: ES 0.24 (1 set) -> 0.34 (2-3) -> 0.44 (4-6).
- Used in: [volume-landmarks.md](volume-landmarks.md)
- https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/20300012/

**Renaissance Periodization** — Volume landmarks framework (MEV/MAV/MRV). Practitioner framework based on coaching data and literature synthesis by Mike Israetel. Not a meta-analysis.
- Used in: [volume-landmarks.md](volume-landmarks.md)
- https://rpstrength.com/blogs/articles/training-volume-landmarks-muscle-growth

## Proximity to Failure

**Refalo et al. 2023** — "Influence of Resistance Training Proximity-to-Failure on Skeletal Muscle Hypertrophy." *Sports Medicine*, 53. **Systematic review with meta-analysis** (15 studies). Failure vs non-failure ES = 0.12 (not significant). Sets need ~0-3 RIR for meaningful stimulus.
- Used in: [muscle-mapping.md](muscle-mapping.md)
- https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/36334240/

**Robinson, Pelland, Remmert, Refalo et al. 2024** — "Exploring the Dose-Response Between Proximity to Failure, Strength Gain, and Hypertrophy." *Sports Medicine*. **Meta-regression**. Hypertrophy increases closer to failure (nonlinear, diminishing returns). For strength, RIR slopes contained zero — negligible relationship.
- Used in: [muscle-mapping.md](muscle-mapping.md)
- https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s40279-024-02069-2

## EMG / Muscle Activation

**Martins-Costa et al. 2020** — "EMG activity in deadlift exercise and its variants." *PLOS ONE*. **Systematic review** (20 studies). Erector spinae highest-activated; significant upper back activation.
- Used in: [muscle-mapping.md](muscle-mapping.md)
- https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7046193/

**Neto et al. 2020** — "Gluteus Maximus Activation during Common Strength and Hypertrophy Exercises." *IJEP*. **Systematic review** (23 studies). Back squat glute activation 28-60% MVIC.
- Used in: [muscle-mapping.md](muscle-mapping.md)
- https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7039033/

**Kompf & Arandjelovic 2024** — "A Biomechanical Review of the Squat Exercise." **Narrative review**. Quads 21-68% MVIC; glute activation depth-dependent.
- Used in: [muscle-mapping.md](muscle-mapping.md)
- https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10987311/

**Lauver et al. 2020** — "Effect of Five Bench Inclinations on EMG Activity." *IJERPH*. Pec major, anterior deltoid, and triceps across angles.
- Used in: [muscle-mapping.md](muscle-mapping.md)
- https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7579505/

**Stastny et al. 2017** — "EMG analyses of the bench press movement task." *PLOS ONE*. **Systematic review**.
- Used in: [muscle-mapping.md](muscle-mapping.md)
- https://journals.plos.org/plosone/article?id=10.1371/journal.pone.0171632

**Coratella et al. 2023** — "EMG of Pectoralis Major during Bench Press Variants." *Applied Sciences*. **Systematic review with meta-analysis**.
- Used in: [muscle-mapping.md](muscle-mapping.md)

**PMC 2019** — "Calculating Set-Volume for Limb Muscles with Multi-Joint Exercises." Methodology for fractional volume attribution from compound movements.

## Sex Differences

**Roberts et al. 2020** — "Sex Differences in Resistance Training." *JSCR*, 34(5). **Systematic review with meta-analysis**. Hypertrophy: no significant sex difference (ES = 0.07). Relative upper-body strength: female advantage (ES = -0.60).
- Used in: [sex-differences.md](sex-differences.md)
- https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/32218059/

**Hicks et al. 2018** — "Sex Differences in Temporal Recovery of Neuromuscular Function." *Frontiers in Physiology*. Women recover faster between sets; but lower-body between-session recovery may be slower (men -10% vs women -20% CMJ at 24h).
- Used in: [sex-differences.md](sex-differences.md)
- https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/physiology/articles/10.3389/fphys.2018.01480/full

**Nuckols et al. 2025** — "Effects of biological sex on fatigue during and recovery from resistance exercise." *PeerJ*. Females completed ~2x total reps in fatigue protocol with lower blood lactate.
- Used in: [sex-differences.md](sex-differences.md)
- https://peerj.com/articles/20542/

**RP Strength** — Glute Hypertrophy Training Guide. Female lifters often doing nearly double male glute volume.
- Used in: [volume-landmarks.md](volume-landmarks.md)
- https://rpstrength.com/blogs/articles/glute-hypertrophy-training-tips

**Stronger By Science** — Strength Training for Women. Women can handle more relative training volume.
- Used in: [sex-differences.md](sex-differences.md)
- https://www.strongerbyscience.com/strength-training-women/

## Menstrual Cycle

**McNulty et al. 2020** — "Effects of Menstrual Cycle Phase on Exercise Performance." *Sports Medicine*, 50(10). **Systematic review with meta-analysis** (78 studies). Overall effect trivial (ES = -0.06). Early follicular vs late follicular largest differential (ES = -0.14). Only 8% of studies rated "high" quality.
- Used in: [adjustments.md](adjustments.md)
- https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/32661839/

**Hayashida et al. 2024** — "Menstrual Cycle Phases on Maximal Strength Performance." *Sports*. **Systematic review with meta-analysis** (22 studies, 433 subjects). Late follicular = peak isometric strength (SMD 0.60 vs early follicular). Dynamic/isokinetic: smaller, less consistent effects.
- Used in: [adjustments.md](adjustments.md)
- https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10818650/

**Colenso-Semple et al. 2023** — Umbrella review. "Premature to conclude that short-term fluctuations in reproductive hormones appreciably influence acute exercise performance or adaptations."
- Used in: [adjustments.md](adjustments.md)
- https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/sports-and-active-living/articles/10.3389/fspor.2023.1054542/full

## Rest Periods

**Schoenfeld et al. 2016** — "Longer Interset Rest Periods Enhance Muscle Strength and Hypertrophy." *JSCR*, 30(7). **RCT** (n=21). 3-minute > 1-minute for both strength (1RM) and muscle thickness in trained men.
- Used in: [periodization.md](periodization.md)
- https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/26605807/

**Grgic et al. 2017** — "Short versus long inter-set rest intervals: effects on muscle hypertrophy." *European Journal of Sport Science*. **Systematic review** (6 studies). Longer rest advantageous for trained individuals.
- Used in: [periodization.md](periodization.md)
- https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/28641044/

**Azevedo et al. 2024** — "Effect of inter-set rest interval duration on muscle hypertrophy." *Frontiers in Sports and Active Living*. **Systematic review with Bayesian meta-analysis**. Small benefit to >60s rest; no appreciable difference above 90s for hypertrophy. For strength: 3-5 minutes well-supported for compound lifts at high intensity.
- Used in: [periodization.md](periodization.md)
- https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11349676/

## Strength-Specific Volume

**Ralston et al. 2017** — "Weekly Training Frequency Effects on Strength Gain: A Meta-Analysis." *Sports Medicine*. **Meta-analysis**. High weekly sets (10+) significantly better than low (<=5) for multi-joint strength (ES=0.18, p=0.04). Recommends 5+ sets/exercise/week for trained lifters.
- Used in: [periodization.md](periodization.md)
- https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC5684266/

**Androulakis-Korakakis et al. 2021** — "Minimum Effective Training Dose for Strength in Powerlifters." *Frontiers in Sports and Active Living*. **Bayesian analysis**. 3-6 working sets/week at 1-5 reps, RPE 7.5-9.5 produces meaningful gains. Back-off sets significantly more effective than single top sets (99.6% probability).
- Used in: [periodization.md](periodization.md)
- https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8435792/
