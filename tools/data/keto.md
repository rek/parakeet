# Keto

## What keto is

**Goal.** Sustain nutritional ketosis — the metabolic state where carb
intake is low enough that the liver produces ketone bodies
(β-hydroxybutyrate, acetoacetate) as the dominant fuel instead of
glucose.

**Mechanism.** Very-low-carb + high-fat intake suppresses insulin,
depletes liver glycogen, and forces fatty-acid oxidation. Typical macro
split: ~70–75% calories from fat, ~20–25% protein, ~5–10% carbs (often
≤20–50g net carbs/day). Blood ketones ≥0.5 mmol/L ≈ "in ketosis".

**Why it's in this research set.** Keto has the strongest published
evidence for lipedema symptom reduction of any diet to date — notably the
22-month follow-up study *Management of Lipedema with Ketogenic Diet*
(Cannataro et al., 2021). Low-glycemic / ketogenic patterns reduce the
insulin and inflammation signals that appear to worsen Rare Adipose
Disorders. Keto is the aggressive lever; RAD is the sustainable one.

## Macro targets

| Lever | Target | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Total carbs | ≤50g/day | Ceiling. Some tolerate 75g once fat-adapted. |
| Net carbs | ≤20g/day | Fiber subtracted. Net is the insulin-relevant signal. |
| Protein | 1.2–1.6 g per kg **lean mass** | Not total body weight. Excess → gluconeogenesis → kicks you out of ketosis. |
| Fat | Fill the rest | ~70–75% of calories. Drives satiety; not a calorie target. |
| Calories | Don't chase a deficit first 6 weeks | Let ketosis stabilize, then adjust if weight stalls. |

Lipedema-specific note: lean-mass estimation is unreliable on affected
limbs because pathological adipose is not ordinary adipose. A DEXA scan
or visual estimate based on unaffected body areas is closer to right than
a bioimpedance-scale readout.

## Core rules

- **Fatty proteins unrestricted**: eggs, fatty fish, poultry with skin,
  buffalo meat, goat, chevon. Organ meats encouraged weekly.
- **High-fat dairy**: butter, ghee, heavy cream, hard aged cheese, yak
  cheese. Milk-as-a-drink, low-fat dairy, flavored/sweetened dairy
  excluded.
- **Fruit**: mostly excluded. Berries (≤100g/day), lemon/lime, avocado
  only. Dates, banana, mango, grapes out.
- **Grains and legumes**: excluded almost entirely. A few legume rows
  allowed in small portions (≤30g cooked).
- **Soy**: tempeh yes (small portions, fermented); tofu cautioned
  (phytoestrogen response).
- **Whey cautioned** pending cleaner replacement (insulinogenic; sucralose
  concern in Nepali brands).
- **Sweeteners**: stevia, erythritol, monk fruit OK. Maltitol causes
  gastric issues and spikes glucose — avoid. Sucralose/aspartame out
  (endocrine + gut effects, also excluded by RAD rules).

## Electrolytes — non-optional on keto

In the first 1–2 weeks glycogen depletion drops body water by 1–3 kg and
with it a lot of sodium, potassium, and magnesium. The cluster of
headaches / fatigue / cramps / brain fog / nausea = "keto flu", and is
almost always an electrolyte deficit rather than a real keto reaction.

**Daily targets while in ketosis** (higher than standard DRIs):

| Mineral | Daily | Primary source |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Sodium | 3000–5000 mg | Pink salt in water, salted broth, liberal salting of food. |
| Potassium | 3500–4700 mg | Avocado, leafy greens, mushrooms, salmon. Supplement if low. |
| Magnesium | 300–500 mg elemental | Glycinate preferred (calming); citrate acceptable. |

Morning ritual: ¼ tsp pink/sea salt in a glass of water on waking. One of
the cheapest, highest-leverage interventions on the whole protocol.

## Practical constraints

- Fiber: aim 25–35 g/day from non-starchy veg (leafy greens, cruciferous,
  avocado). Constipation is the second-most-common keto complaint after
  electrolyte issues.
- Hydration: water + herbal teas. Coffee OK, ≤3 cups, no sugar. Alcohol
  halts fat oxidation while metabolized — keep rare.
- Fats to favor: olive oil, ghee, butter (A2 where possible), coconut
  oil, avocado oil. Omega-6 seed oils (soybean, sunflower, corn, canola)
  should be minimized — they track with inflammation and pro-edema
  eicosanoid pathways, which matters extra for lipedema.
- MCT oil optional, not required. Starts at ½ tsp and builds to ≤1 tbsp
  at a time — GI upset is dose-dependent.

## Common failure modes

Knowing these up front is 80% of the durable-keto problem.

- **Keto flu (weeks 1–2).** Electrolytes, electrolytes, electrolytes. Pre-emptive
  sodium loading prevents most of it.
- **Protein creep.** "Lean keto" (high protein, moderate fat) converts excess
  protein to glucose via gluconeogenesis. Symptom: stuck at 0.3 mmol/L
  no matter how strict carbs are. Fix: raise fat, recheck protein math.
- **Hidden carbs.** Nepali yoghurt/achar/daal-bhat carb loads are larger
  than they look. Street food sauces/chutneys are routinely
  sugar-sweetened. Assume ≥5g hidden per restaurant meal.
- **Plateau at 8–12 weeks.** Expected. Usually means ketosis has
  normalised and the body has adapted. Don't drop carbs further first —
  try a 24–48h fast, a refeed + re-entry, or a 2-week break. Constant
  deeper restriction breaks things.
- **Hair shedding (month 3–4).** Transient telogen effluvium from the
  metabolic shift. Usually resolves in 1–2 months. Adequate protein,
  biotin-rich eggs, and iron status help.
- **Gallbladder pain / discomfort.** Sudden high-fat intake can provoke
  symptoms in people with existing sludge. Ramp fat gradually; add ox
  bile or lipase enzymes short-term if needed; see a doctor for actual
  pain.
- **Thyroid lethargy.** Prolonged very-low-carb can drop T3 (a
  physiological adaptation, not necessarily pathological). If fatigue /
  cold intolerance sets in: add a weekly carb refeed (100–150g from
  low-GI whole sources), check selenium and iodine intake, get TSH/T3
  tested.
- **Periodic reseeding / cycle.** Menstrual-cycle carb tolerance varies;
  some feel better with a small refeed in the luteal phase. Track and
  adjust.

## Lipedema-specific adjustments

- **Run keto *alongside* lymphatic lifestyle (compression, MLD,
  rebounding)** — diet doesn't shrink pathological adipose on its own.
  See RAD lifestyle rows.
- **Keep sodium generous even though RAD historically recommends
  low-sodium.** Keto sodium loss overrides the edema concern for most
  patients. If leg swelling worsens on added salt, back off and
  prioritize potassium.
- **Watch inflammation markers** rather than scale weight. Pain 0–10,
  swelling 0–10, limb circumference, and sleep quality matter more than
  kg on lipedema limbs.
- **Red meat is permissible** under keto-for-lipedema literature (lean
  cuts, not processed), diverging from Herbst's RAD baseline. See
  `rad.md` for the full contested-protein discussion.

## Nepal local adaptations

- **A2 dairy via buffalo & yak.** Cow ghee/butter usually A1; buffalo ghee
  and yak-milk derivatives are the go-to fat sources.
- **Fats available locally.** Ghee, mustard oil (use cold — smoke point
  issue), coconut oil. Olive oil is import-only and expensive; use where
  raw/low-heat use makes it worth the cost.
- **Meats.** Buffalo and goat/chevon are the practical red-meat choices.
  Pork availability varies by region; fish is regional (more available
  in Terai lowlands).
- **Vegetable basket.** Spinach (palak), fenugreek leaves (methi saag),
  mustard greens (rayo), cauliflower, cabbage, okra, radish, bitter
  gourd (karela) all fit keto well. Potato, sweet potato, taro out.
- **Watch-outs in typical Nepali diet.** Rice, roti, chiura, dal-bhat
  portions, sweet chai, achar with sugar, momos wrappers. These are the
  main blockers to keto adherence for anyone switching from a
  conventional Nepali diet.

## Evidence quality

| Claim | Grade | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Keto reduces lipedema pain / swelling | **A** | RCT (Cannataro 2021, 70 women, 8 weeks); 22-month follow-up showed sustained effect; 2024 Nutrients systematic review + meta-analysis; 2025 Current Obesity Reports narrative review. |
| Electrolyte supplementation prevents keto flu | **A** | Mechanism direct; clinically obvious; not RCT'd but not really contested. |
| Specific macro split (70/25/5) | **B** | Works clinically; individual variation large. |
| Omega-3 fish oil helps inflammation on top of keto | **B** | Generally accepted; lipedema-specific data thin. |
| MCT oil accelerates ketosis | **B** | Causes faster BHB rise; whether it improves outcomes beyond that is weaker. |
| Exogenous ketones substitute for the diet | **C** | Don't. Raising BHB without insulin/carb control misses the mechanism. |

## What this protocol does NOT claim

Keto is not a cure for lipedema. No diet shrinks pathological adipose
tissue. Symptom reduction (pain, swelling, inflammation), slowed
progression, and weight normalisation on the non-pathological body
compartment are the realistic goals. Definitive treatment for abnormal
adipose remains surgical (lymph-sparing liposuction).
