# Keto

**Goal.** Sustain nutritional ketosis — the metabolic state where carb
intake is low enough that the liver produces ketone bodies (β-hydroxybutyrate,
acetoacetate) as the dominant fuel instead of glucose.

**Mechanism.** Very-low-carb + high-fat intake suppresses insulin, depletes
liver glycogen, and forces fatty-acid oxidation. Typical macro split:
~70–75% calories from fat, ~20–25% protein, ~5–10% carbs (often ≤20–50g
net carbs/day). Blood ketones ≥0.5 mmol/L ≈ "in ketosis".

**Why it's in this research set.** Keto has the strongest published evidence
for lipedema symptom reduction of any diet to date — notably the 22-month
follow-up study *Management of Lipedema with Ketogenic Diet* (Cannataro et
al., 2021). Low-glycemic/ketogenic patterns reduce the insulin and
inflammation signals that appear to worsen Rare Adipose Disorders. Keto is
the aggressive lever; RAD is the sustainable one.

## Core rules

- **Fatty proteins unrestricted**: eggs, fatty fish, poultry, buffalo meat.
- **High-fat dairy**: butter, heavy cream, hard aged cheese, yak cheese.
  Milk-as-a-drink, low-fat dairy, flavored/sweetened dairy excluded.
- **Fruit**: mostly excluded; berries and a few low-carb fruits only.
- **Grains and legumes**: excluded almost entirely (a few legume rows
  allowed in small portions).
- **Soy**: tempeh yes (small portions); tofu cautioned (phytoestrogen
  response).
- **Whey cautioned** pending cleaner replacement (sucralose concern).

## Practical constraints

- Carb ceiling: keep total carbs ≤50g/day, net carbs ≤20g/day for deeper
  ketosis.
- Protein: ~1.2–1.6g per kg lean mass. Too much becomes gluconeogenic.
- Electrolytes: sodium, potassium, magnesium drop fast in first 1–2 weeks
  ("keto flu"). Supplement.
- Fiber from non-starchy veg (leafy greens, cruciferous, avocado).
