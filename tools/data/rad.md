# RAD — Rare Adipose Disorders Diet

## What RAD stands for

*Rare Adipose Disorders* — an umbrella term for conditions involving
abnormal subcutaneous adipose tissue that are routinely misdiagnosed as
ordinary obesity. The three primary RADs are:

| Disorder | Description |
| --- | --- |
| **Lipedema** | Symmetric, progressive, painful fat accumulation in the legs/hips (and often arms), sparing the feet/hands. Predominantly affects women; hormonally triggered (puberty, pregnancy, menopause). |
| **Dercum's Disease (DD)** | Adiposis dolorosa — multiple painful lipomas, chronic pain, fatigue, cognitive symptoms. |
| **Multiple Symmetric Lipomatosis (MSL)** | Symmetric benign lipomas (neck, shoulders, trunk); can occur with or without HIV. Madelung's disease variant. |

The defining clinical fact: **these disorders do not respond to caloric
restriction or bariatric surgery for the abnormal-fat component.** Lifestyle
changes help the comorbid ordinary obesity, but the pathological adipose
tissue persists. Pathophysiology involves vascular/lymphatic dysfunction
and (for MSL) aberrant brown-stem-cell growth — not caloric imbalance.

**This protocol is focused on lipedema specifically.**

## Who developed the RAD diet

Dr Karen L. Herbst, PhD, MD — endocrinologist and the leading clinical
researcher on RADs. The diet is a Mediterranean-style pattern modified for
low glycemic load, anti-inflammatory action, and lymphatic support.

## Goal of the diet

1. Keep insulin low — insulin promotes adipose growth, and RAD fat is
   already dysregulated.
2. Reduce systemic and lymphatic inflammation.
3. Support lymphatic drainage (bioflavonoids, enzymes from fresh produce).
4. Avoid foods that aggravate gut permeability, casein/gluten sensitivity,
   or endocrine disruption.

## Core rules

- **Low glycemic index.** No refined sugar, no refined flour, no white
  rice/potatoes/pasta/bread. Limit high-GI fruit; favor berries, citrus,
  pomegranate.
- **Anti-inflammatory.** Heavy on omega-3 (fatty fish), leafy greens,
  cruciferous veg, olive oil, turmeric, ginger. Avoid seed oils high in
  omega-6 (soybean, corn, sunflower).
- **Whole foods only.** No preservatives, artificial sweeteners (especially
  aspartame), colors, stabilizers, processed/packaged/fast food.
- **Gluten-free.** Wheat, barley, rye out. Gluten-free whole grains
  (quinoa, buckwheat, oats if certified GF) may be allowed in moderation.
- **Dairy with care.** Standard RAD guidance limits pasteurized cow dairy.
  This protocol goes further — A2 sources preferred, A1 cautioned.
- **Low salt.** Sodium promotes fluid retention, which worsens lymphatic
  congestion.
- **Hydration.** Water + herbal teas; minimal alcohol, no sweetened drinks.

### Protein choices — contested

- **Herbst / Mediterranean camp**: avoid red meat, favor fish + plant
  proteins. Rationale: saturated fat → inflammation, and lipedema is an
  inflammatory disease.
- **Keto-for-lipedema camp** (Cannataro 2021, Nutrients 2024 systematic
  review, Current Obesity Reports 2025 narrative review): red meat is
  *permitted* in moderation, lean cuts preferred. Rationale: the
  insulin/glycemic lever dominates — carb control matters more than
  protein source.
- **Consensus across both camps**: processed meats out (bacon, sausage,
  deli, sugar-marinated, nitrate-preserved). Lean cuts preferred.
- **This protocol's stance**: buffalo meat (leaner than beef) allowed
  freely; standard red meat cuts not listed; processed meats excluded.
  Defensible under the keto-lipedema literature; divergent from Herbst's
  original guidance.

## Observable rules from the CSV

- A2 dairy preferred: buffalo, yak, goat, sheep. A1-risk cow dairy
  cautioned or excluded. Fermentation favored (kefir, yoghurt, aged
  cheeses) because fermentation reduces problematic casein and supports
  gut/lymphatic health.
- Tempeh preferred over tofu (fermented > unfermented soy).
- Whey cautioned (insulinogenic, IGF-1 concern) — replace with pea/brown
  rice protein.
- Heavily processed meats / sugar-heavy marinades excluded.

Two categories unique to this protocol reflect RAD-specific daily rituals:

- **`rad_superfoods`** — concentrated anti-inflammatory / lymphatic foods
  eaten daily or near-daily: bitter gourd (karela), fennel, pomegranate,
  goji berries, bee pollen, turmeric + black pepper (curcumin
  bioavailability), moringa, mushrooms, berries, green tea, dark leafy
  greens, ashwagandha.
- **`morning_shot`** — a prescribed daily anti-inflammatory drink:
  turmeric, moringa, kale powder, ashwagandha, goji powder, fresh ginger,
  black pepper, fresh lemon juice. Taken on waking before food.

## Nepal local adaptation

The protein and dairy rows reference buffalo meat, buffalo milk paneer,
yak cheese, and the A1/A2 casein distinction — a Nepal-/South-Asia-context
adaptation where buffalo is the locally available A2 source. Buffalo meat
is leaner than beef and fits the "lean red meat in moderation" position of
the keto-for-lipedema literature, even though it diverges from Herbst's
red-meat-avoidant baseline. Divergences are kept explicit in CSV `notes`
rows.

## Lifestyle components

Diet alone isn't the whole protocol.

- **Compression garments** for lipedema limbs.
- **Manual lymphatic drainage** (MLD) massage — weekly or self-administered.
- **Low-impact movement**: swimming, walking, rebounding, cycling. Heavy
  impact aggravates inflamed tissue.
- **Stress management** — cortisol worsens inflammation and fluid
  retention.
- **Sleep hygiene** — lymphatic clearance peaks during deep sleep.

## Evidence quality

- **Strongest evidence**: low-GI / ketogenic patterns for lipedema symptom
  reduction. RCT (70 women, 8 weeks) showed superior pain relief on
  low-carb vs control. 22-month follow-up study showed sustained weight,
  BMI, and pain improvements. Systematic review + meta-analysis (Nutrients
  2024) and narrative review (Current Obesity Reports 2025) both support
  ketogenic intervention as effective for lipedema.
- **Weaker evidence**: specific supplement doses (D3, selenium, diosmin,
  butcher's broom, rutin). Mostly anecdotal / small retrospective cases.
  Not harmful at reasonable doses, but not RCT-validated.
- **Weakest evidence**: A1/A2 casein distinction as a disease driver. The
  A2-milk hypothesis has commercial backing but mixed peer-reviewed
  support; its relevance to lipedema specifically is unstudied. Treat the
  A2 preference as a personal sensitivity adaptation, not a RAD-protocol
  requirement.
- **Contested**: red meat avoidance (see "Protein choices" above).

## What this diet does NOT claim

RAD diet is not a cure for lipedema, DD, or MSL. No diet shrinks
pathological adipose tissue. The goal is symptom reduction: less pain, less
swelling, slowed progression, reduced inflammation, and management of the
comorbid ordinary obesity component. Definitive treatment for the abnormal
adipose remains surgical (lymph-sparing liposuction).
